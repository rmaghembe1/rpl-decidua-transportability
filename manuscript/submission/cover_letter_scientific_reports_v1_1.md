Please consider our manuscript entitled “Single-cell-informed transcriptomic transportability of decidual inflammatory macrophage/APC programmes in recurrent pregnancy loss” for publication as a Research Article in Scientific Reports.

Recurrent pregnancy loss remains biologically heterogeneous, and many cases remain unexplained after standard clinical evaluation. Although bulk transcriptomic studies of recurrent pregnancy loss have reported immune and inflammatory signals, interpretation is often limited by small cohorts, tissue-composition effects and dataset-specific gene lists. In this study, we address this problem using a single-cell-informed transcriptomic transportability framework rather than a conventional diagnostic-classifier or single-cohort differential-expression design.

Using GSE214607 as a disease single-cell/pseudobulk reference, we derived compact, interpretable recurrent-miscarriage-associated decidual and tissue-state programmes. We then projected these programmes into three independent bulk decidua transcriptomic cohorts: GSE161969, GSE113790 and GSE178535. The most reproducible finding was a recurrent-miscarriage decidual inflammatory macrophage/APC programme that showed complete 30-gene coverage in all three validation cohorts and positive case-associated shifts in all three datasets. The cohort-level deltas were +1.019, +0.282 and +0.941, giving a mean delta of +0.747 and directional recurrence in 3/3 independent cohorts.

The manuscript’s main contribution is therefore not a standalone biomarker claim, but a reproducible programme-level observation: a single-cell-derived decidual inflammatory macrophage/APC tissue-state axis is directionally transportable across independent recurrent pregnancy loss decidua datasets. We believe this conservative framing will be of interest to readers working in reproductive biology, maternal-fetal-interface immunology, pregnancy loss, transcriptomics and computational reanalysis of public omics datasets.

The study uses only publicly available, de-identified datasets and does not involve newly recruited participants or newly generated identifiable human data. All analysis outputs, derived signatures, curated summaries, manuscript files and reproducibility documentation are available through the associated GitHub repository. The manuscript explicitly avoids causal, diagnostic-performance or clinical-utility overclaiming and frames the findings as exploratory, observational and hypothesis-generating.

The manuscript is original, is not under consideration elsewhere and has not been published previously. The author declares no competing interests.
